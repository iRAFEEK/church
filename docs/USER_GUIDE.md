# Part I — Welcome

## 1. What is Ekklesia?

Ekklesia is a church management app built for Arabic-speaking churches, with full English support. It brings everything a church community does into one place:

- **For members:** see events and RSVP, sign up to serve, read announcements, share prayer requests, read the Bible and liturgy, book church rooms, and stay connected.
- **For group leaders:** run your small group — schedule gatherings, take attendance, track prayer requests, and follow up with visitors assigned to you.
- **For pastors and admins:** manage the whole church — members, visitors, groups, ministries, events, serving schedules, announcements, and church-wide notifications.

Ekklesia works in any modern web browser, on desktop and on phones. It's a *Progressive Web App* (PWA), which means you can install it on your phone's home screen and it even shows a friendly offline page when you have no internet.

**Arabic comes first.** The entire app works in Arabic (right-to-left) and English, and you can switch between them with one tap at any time (see [section 9](#9-switching-between-arabic-and-english)).

## 2. The four roles — and what each one can do

Everyone in Ekklesia has one of four roles **per church**. Your role decides which menus you see and what you're allowed to do.

| Role (as shown in the app) | Who it's for | What they see |
|---|---|---|
| **Member** | Everyone in the congregation | Dashboard, My Profile, Notifications, Events, Serving, Announcements, Prayer Requests, Songs, Bible, Liturgy, Church Needs, Room Booking |
| **Group Leader** | Leads a small group | Everything a Member sees, **plus**: My Group (gatherings, attendance, group prayers) and My Visitors (visitors assigned to them) |
| **Ministry Leader** | Runs ministries and day-to-day church operations | Everything above, **plus** the admin sections: Members, Visitors Queue, Join Requests, Groups, Ministries, Outreach, Calendar, Templates, Prayer Management, Send Notifications |
| **Admin** (super admin) | The pastor / main administrator | Everything, **plus** Settings (QR Code, Role Permissions, Privacy, Notifications) and per-member permission overrides |

Two more "roles" exist outside the app itself:

- **Visitor** — someone who scanned the church's QR code and filled in the welcome form. They don't have an account yet.
- **Platform operator** — the person who approves brand-new churches onto the platform (usually the Ekklesia team).

> 💡 **Good to know:** admins can fine-tune what each role is allowed to do, and even grant extra permissions to a single person — see [section 52](#52-role-permissions--per-member-overrides).

# Part II — Getting Started

## 3. Registering your church (for pastors)

If your church isn't on Ekklesia yet, you (the pastor or main admin) register it once. The whole thing takes about 3 minutes.

1. Open the Ekklesia website. You'll land on a welcome page with the verse *"Feed my sheep."*
2. Click the black **Register Your Church** button in the top-right corner (it also appears further down the page).
3. You'll see a full-screen wizard that says **"Let's set up your church"** with a progress label — **"Step 1 of 7"** — at the top. Three highlights are listed: **Member & Visitor Management**, **Groups & Attendance Tracking**, and **Events, Serving & Communication**. Click **Let's Go**.
4. **Create your admin account.** Fill in:
   - **Email address** — e.g. `pastor@church.com`. This will be your sign-in email.
   - **Password** — "At least 6 characters".
   - **Confirm password** — type it again. If they don't match you'll see *"Passwords don't match"*.
   - **Your name** — e.g. "Pastor John Samaan".
   - **Phone number** — e.g. `+20 100 123 4567`. The review team may contact you on this number to verify the church.
   Click **Continue**.
5. **"What's your church called?"** Enter:
   - **Church name in Arabic** — e.g. كنيسة النعمة.
   - **Church name in English** — marked **Optional**.
   A live **Preview** underneath shows exactly how the name will appear in the app. Click **Continue**.
6. **"A few quick details."** Choose:
   - **Where is your church?** (country)
   - **Timezone**
   - **Primary language** — **Arabic** or **English**
   - **Denomination**
   - **Default Bible translation**
   Click **Continue**.
7. **"Welcome your visitors."** Write a short welcome message that visitors will see when they scan your church's QR code (e.g. "Welcome to our church! We're glad you're here..."). A preview titled **"How visitors will see it"** appears as you type. Click **Continue** — or **Skip for now**.
8. **"Add your church leaders."** Optionally add leaders (name + title in Arabic and English, e.g. "Pastor George — Senior Pastor") who will appear on your church's public page. Click **Add a leader** to add each one, then **Continue** — or **I'll add leaders later**.
9. The final screen confirms your setup with a summary (**Church**, **Admin**, **Country**) and a **"What's next?"** list: *Invite your team members*, *Create your first group*, *Print your visitor QR code*.

**What happens next — important:** your church is created in a **pending** state. Instead of the dashboard you'll see a page titled **"Your church is under review"** explaining that the team is reviewing your registration and will activate it shortly. This is normal! You can **Sign out** and come back later. Once an operator approves your church (usually quickly), signing in takes you straight to your admin dashboard with full access.

> ⚠️ **Common mistake:** the email you use here becomes the church's first **Admin** account. Use a real address you control — password reset emails go there.

## 4. Creating an account & joining a church (for members)

If your church is already on Ekklesia, you can create your own account:

1. On the sign-in page, click **Create an account** (next to "New to Ekklesia?").
2. You'll see **"Create your account"** — *"Join your church community on Ekklesia"*. First, find your church:
   - Type in **"Search by church name…"**. Matching churches appear as you type.
   - Pick your church. The form confirms **"Signing up for {your church}"** — if you picked the wrong one, click **Change**.
   - If nothing matches, you'll see *"No churches found"* with a link to **Register a new church**.
3. Enter your **Email**, **Password** (at least 6 characters), and **Confirm password**.
4. Click **Create account**. On success you'll see *"Account created successfully!"*
5. You'll be taken through first-time onboarding to complete your profile ([section 7](#7-completing-your-profile--first-time-onboarding)).

**How approval works:** the *first* church you join during sign-up welcomes you immediately. If you later ask to join an *additional* church (see [section 11](#11-belonging-to-more-than-one-church)), that request goes to the church's leaders for approval first.

**Were you added by your leader?** Church leaders can pre-add people by name and phone number. If that's you, you don't need to sign up at all — just sign in with your **Phone** using the WhatsApp code ([section 5](#5-signing-in)) and your account is automatically claimed and activated, with your name already filled in.

## 5. Signing in

1. Go to the app. If you're not signed in, you'll land on the **Sign In** page — *"Enter your email and password to sign in"*.
2. Notice the two tabs at the top of the card: **Email** and **Phone**.

**Signing in with email:**
1. On the **Email** tab, enter your **Email** and **Password**.
2. Click **Sign In**. While it works you'll see *"Signing in..."*.
3. If something's wrong you'll get a *"Login failed"* message — check your email and password.

**Signing in with your phone (WhatsApp code):**
1. Switch to the **Phone** tab.
2. Enter your **Phone number** in international format — the hint reads *"Include your country code, e.g. +20 for Egypt."*
3. Click **Send code via WhatsApp**.
4. You'll see **"Code sent"** — *"Check WhatsApp for your 6-digit code."* Open WhatsApp on your phone; the code arrives as a message.
5. Type the 6 digits into **Verification code** and click **Verify and sign in**.
6. Typed the wrong number? Click **Change number** to go back.

**Where you land after signing in:**
- Most people go straight to their **Dashboard**.
- First-time users are taken to onboarding ([section 7](#7-completing-your-profile--first-time-onboarding)).
- If you belong to **more than one church**, you'll first see **"Choose a church"** — pick the one you want to work in ("You can switch anytime from the app").

> ⚠️ If the code doesn't arrive: *"The code is incorrect or expired. Please try again."* means exactly that — request a fresh code. Codes are single-use and expire quickly.

## 6. Forgot your password?

1. On the **Sign In** page (Email tab), click **Forgot password?** just under the Sign In button.
2. You'll see **"Reset your password"** — *"Enter your email and we'll send you a link to reset your password."*
3. Type your **Email** and click **Send reset link**.
4. The page will confirm: *"If an account exists for that email, we've sent a password reset link. Check your inbox."* (For your privacy, this message is shown whether or not the email exists.)
5. Open the email and click the link. You'll land on **"Set a new password"**.
6. Enter your **New password** and **Confirm password**, then click **Update password**.
7. On success — *"Your password has been updated."* — you're signed in and taken into the app.

> ⚠️ If you see **"Link expired or invalid"**, the link is too old or was already used. Click the button on that page to request a new one from **Reset your password**.

## 7. Completing your profile — first-time onboarding

The first time you sign in, Ekklesia asks for a few details. You'll see a warm **"Welcome"** screen with a card titled **"Complete Your Profile"** — *"We need some information to set up your account. All information is confidential."*

1. **Name in Arabic** (required) — **First Name** and **Last Name** (e.g. يوحنا الحنا).
2. **Name in English (Optional)** — First and Last name in English.
3. **Phone** — with your country code (e.g. +961 XX XXX XXX). The note explains it *"Will be used to send WhatsApp notifications"*.
4. **Gender** — **Male** or **Female**.
5. **Date of Birth** and **Occupation** (e.g. "Engineer, Student, Teacher...").
6. **Notification Preference** — how you'd like to be reached: **WhatsApp**, **SMS**, **Email**, **All**, or **None**.
7. Click **Get Started →**.
8. You'll see *"Welcome!" — "Your profile has been created successfully"* and land on your dashboard.

> 💡 If a leader pre-added you, your name and phone are already filled in — just review and continue. Nothing you were given gets erased.

## 8. Finding your way around

**On a computer** the app has three parts:

- **Sidebar (left)** — the main menu, organized in sections: **Main** (Dashboard, My Profile, Notifications), **Groups**, **People**, **Ministry**, **Resources**, and **Admin** (what you see depends on your role). At the very top is your church's name; at the bottom, your own name and **Sign out**. You can collapse the sidebar with the small arrow (**Collapse sidebar** / **Expand sidebar**).
- **Top bar** — shows the church name, the language toggle (**عر** / **EN**), the notification bell 🔔 with a red unread-count badge, and your avatar (the **User menu**, with **My Profile** and **Edit Profile**).
- **Main area** — the page you're on.

**On a phone** the sidebar is replaced by a **bottom navigation bar**: **Home**, **Groups**, **Notifications**, and **More** (leaders also see **Ministries**). Tapping **More** opens the **Menu** sheet with every remaining page, plus **العربية** (language switch) and **Sign Out**.

**Signing out:** click **Sign out** at the bottom of the sidebar (or in the **More** menu on mobile). You'll see a *"Signed out"* confirmation and return to the sign-in page.

## 9. Switching between Arabic and English

- **On a computer:** click the small **عر** button in the top bar (when the app is in Arabic, it shows **EN** instead). The entire app instantly switches — including layout direction: Arabic runs right-to-left, with the sidebar on the right.
- **On a phone:** open **More → العربية** (or **English**).
- **From your profile:** open **My Profile → Edit**, and under **App Language** choose **العربية (Arabic)** or **English** — the note confirms this *"Changes the language of the entire app"*.

Your choice is remembered on that device.

## 10. Turning on push notifications

Shortly after you sign in, a small card appears in the corner: **"Enable push notifications"** — *"Get notified for prayer requests, serving assignments, and events — even when the app is closed."*

- Click **Enable**, then allow notifications when your browser asks. You'll see *"Push notifications enabled"*.
- Not now? Click **Later** — the app works fine without it, and notifications still collect in your in-app **Notifications** page.

> 💡 In-app notifications are always on and free. Push just makes them reach your phone's lock screen too.

## 11. Belonging to more than one church

One Ekklesia account can belong to several churches (for example, you moved cities, or you serve two congregations).

- **Joining another church:** from onboarding's **"Find your church"** search, pick the additional church and click **Join**. Because you already belong to a church, this creates a *request* — the new church's leaders must approve it (they'll see it in their **Join Requests** page). You'll be a member once they approve.
- **Choosing where to sign in:** with multiple churches, signing in shows **"Choose a church"** — pick one. *"You can switch anytime from the app."*
- **Switching later:** click your church's name at the top of the sidebar to open the church switcher and jump between your churches. Your role can be different in each church — you might be an Admin in one and a Member in another.

# Part III — Everyday Member Guide

## 12. Your dashboard

Your dashboard is your home page — what's on it depends on your role. As a **member** you'll see:

- A greeting — **"Welcome, {your name}"** — with your church's name and your role badge (e.g. **Member**).
- Four stat cards: **My Attendance**, **My Milestones**, **My Groups**, and **Notifications** (with an *unread* count).
- **My Groups** — the groups you belong to, with your next gathering date ("Next: …") and the leader's name. If you're not in a group yet: *"You are not in any groups yet"*.
- **Upcoming Events** — the next events at your church; events you've registered for show a **Registered** tag.
- **My Serving** — your upcoming volunteer slots (*"No upcoming serving slots"* when you have none).
- **Recent Announcements** — the latest church news.

Cards have **View All** links that jump to the full pages.

## 13. Your profile

Click **My Profile** in the sidebar.

**What you'll see:** your photo (or initials), your name, role and status badges, when you **Joined**, and four tabs:

- **Info** — your personal details (phone, email, gender…). If you haven't filled anything in you'll see *"No additional information"* with a **Complete your profile** link.
- **Spiritual Milestones** — life events recorded for you: **Baptism**, **Salvation**, **Bible Reading Plan Completed**, **Leadership Training**, **Marriage**, **Other**. (*"No spiritual milestones recorded yet"* if empty.)
- **Attendance** — your **Attendance Record** from group gatherings.
- **Involvement** — everything you take part in (see [section 35](#35-managing-one-members-profile) for the same tab as admins see it).

Leaders also get an **"I Lead"** card listing groups/areas where they're **Leader** or **Co-Leader**.

**Editing your profile:**
1. Click the **Edit** button (top right of your profile).
2. On **Edit Profile** you can change:
   - **Profile Photo** — click **Add Photo** / **Change Photo** and pick an image. It must be a valid image under **5MB** (you'll see *"Image too large (max 5MB)"* otherwise). Success shows *"Photo uploaded"*.
   - **Name in Arabic** (required) and **Name in English**.
   - **Phone**, **Gender**, **Date of Birth**, **Occupation (in Arabic)**.
   - **Notification Preference** — **WhatsApp** / **SMS** / **Email** / **All** / **None**, plus **Push Notifications**.
   - **App Language** — Arabic or English.
3. Click **Save Changes** (or **Cancel**). Success shows *"Changes saved"*.

## 14. Notifications

**The bell 🔔** in the top bar shows a red badge with your unread count. Click it for a quick dropdown of recent notifications; click one to open the related page. **Mark all read** clears the badge.

**The full page** — click **Notifications** in the sidebar:

1. The header shows **Notifications** — *"{count} notifications, {unread} unread"*.
2. Filter with the **type dropdown** (e.g. **Event Reminder**, **Gathering Reminder**, **Announcement**, **Visitor Assigned**…) or the **Unread only** toggle.
3. Unread items show a dot; click any notification to open a detail dialog with its full text and — when relevant — a **View Details** button that jumps to the related event, prayer, or visitor.
4. **Mark all read** (top right) clears everything at once.
5. Long lists are paginated — **Page {x} of {y}** with **Previous page** / **Next page** arrows.

When you're all caught up you'll see **"All caught up"** — *"You have no notifications right now."*

## 15. Events — browsing and registering

Click **Events** in the sidebar.

**What you'll see:** **"Upcoming Events"** — *"Events happening at your church"* — as a grid of cards. Each card shows the event's name, date, time, place, and a type badge (e.g. **Service**, **Conference**, **Youth**). Use the search box (**"Search events..."**) or the **Ministry** / **Group** filters to narrow the list.

**Viewing an event:** click its card. The detail page shows the title, type, date & time, and location.

**Registering (RSVP):** if the event requires registration you'll see a **Register** button.
1. Click **Register**. You'll see *"Registering..."* then *"Registered successfully!"*
2. The button area now says **"You are registered"**.
3. If it's full you'll see **Full** / *"This slot is full"*-style messaging, or **Registration Closed** if the deadline passed. Capacity events show **"{count} spots remaining"**.

> 💡 No **Register** button? The event doesn't need registration — just show up!

## 16. Serving — volunteering for a slot

Click **Serving** in the sidebar. As a member you'll see **"Serving"** — *"Sign up to serve at your church"*.

1. Browse **Open Slots** — each card shows the slot's title, date, time, its serving area, and how many volunteers it has (e.g. **"2 / 5 volunteers"**).
2. Click a slot to see details, then click **Sign Up**.
3. You'll see *"You have signed up successfully"* and the card changes to **"You are signed up!"**
4. Your active slots are listed under **My Sign-ups**.

**Changing your mind:**
1. Open the slot and click **Cancel Sign-up**.
2. A confirmation dialog asks *"Are you sure you want to cancel your sign-up?"* — choose **Cancel Sign-up** to confirm or **Keep Sign-up** to stay.
3. You'll see *"Sign-up cancelled"*.

> ⚠️ A slot showing **Full** has reached its **Max Volunteers** — pick another, or check back later in case someone cancels.

## 17. Announcements

Click **Announcements** in the sidebar to see **"Latest news and updates from the church"**.

- Announcements are cards with a title, body text, and date.
- **Pinned** announcements (marked with a pin) always appear at the top — that's the church's most important news.
- If there's nothing to read: *"No announcements at this time"*.

There's nothing to do here but read — announcements are one-way messages from your church's leaders.

## 18. Prayer requests

Click **Prayer Requests** in the sidebar.

**Submitting a request:**
1. At the top, under **"What would you like prayer for?"**, type your request in the box (*"Share your prayer request here..."*).
2. Choose a **Visibility**:
   - **Share with church** — *"Your name will be visible to the church community"*.
   - **Share anonymously** — *"Your prayer will be shared but your name will be hidden"* (it appears as **"A church member"**).
   - **Private (leaders only)** — *"Only church leaders will see this prayer request"*.
3. Click **Submit Prayer**. You'll see *"Your prayer has been submitted"* and it appears immediately.

**The two tabs:**
- **Church Feed** — everyone's shared prayers, newest first. Under each one is an **I'm Praying** button — tap it to encourage the person; it changes to **Praying** and the card counts *"# people praying"*. Tap again to undo.
- **My Requests** — everything you've submitted, including private ones, with their status (**Answered** prayers are marked).

> 💡 Be the first! An empty feed shows *"Be the first to share a prayer request with your church community."*

## 19. Reading the Bible

Click **Bible** in the sidebar — **"Read, bookmark, and highlight Scripture"**.

**Getting to a chapter:**
1. The first dropdown shows the current **Bible Version** (e.g. Smith & Van Dyke Arabic). Click it to switch translations.
2. Click **Select a Book** — books are grouped into **Old Testament**, **New Testament** (and **Deuterocanonical Books** where the translation has them). Use **"Search books..."** to type-ahead.
3. Click **Select a Chapter** and pick one (e.g. **Chapter 1**). The full chapter appears.

**Working with verses** — click (or press Enter on) any verse to select it. An action bar appears at the top:
- **Highlight** — pick a color: **Yellow**, **Green**, **Blue**, **Pink**, **Orange**. (*"Highlight added"*.) Click the color again to remove it.
- **Bookmark** 🔖 — saves the verse (*"Bookmark added"*). Find all your bookmarks under **Bible → Bookmarks** (*"No bookmarks yet"* when empty).
- **Copy** — copies the verse text with its reference (*"Verse copied to clipboard"*).
- **Share** — opens your device's share menu (or copies the text).
- **X** — closes the action bar.

**Other tools in the toolbar:**
- **Search the Bible** 🔍 — type a word or phrase (*"Search for a word or phrase..."*); results show *"{count} results found"* and clicking one jumps to that verse.
- **Font size** — **Increase font size** / **Decrease font size** buttons.
- **Presenter** — full-screen projection mode for services (see shortcuts in [section 57](#57-keyboard-shortcuts--power-features)).

## 20. Liturgy — Agpeya, readings, hymns and more

Click **Liturgy** in the sidebar — *"Prayers, readings, hymns, and liturgical resources"*. It's organized as cards:

- **Agpeya** — *"The Book of Hours — daily prayers for every hour"*: **First Hour**, **Third Hour**, **Sixth Hour**, **Ninth Hour**, **Eleventh Hour**, **Twelfth Hour**, **Midnight**. The app highlights the **Current hour** for you.
- **Today's Readings** — *"Katameros — daily lectionary readings"*, with the **Coptic date** and **Season**; use **Select date** to read another day. Includes the **Synaxarium**.
- **Divine Liturgy** — full texts: **Liturgy of St. Basil**, **Liturgy of St. Gregory**, **Liturgy of St. Cyril**.
- **Psalmody** — **Annual Psalmody** and **Kiahk Psalmody**.
- **Hymns** — *"Browse and search hymns"* with **"Search hymns..."** and **Filter by season**.
- **Clergy Resources** — visible to clergy only: Baptism, Crowning (Wedding), Funeral, Anointing of the Sick, Holy Week, Raising of Incense, and more.

Inside any text you can switch between **Arabic**, **English**, and **Coptic** where available, **Bookmark** sections, expand with **Show more**, and project with **Presenter Mode**.

## 21. Songs

Click **Songs** in the sidebar to browse the worship song library.

- Use **"Search songs by title, artist, or lyrics..."** — matches inside lyrics show which slide they're on (**"Slide {number}"**).
- Click a song to read its lyrics, shown slide-by-slide the way they'd be projected.
- Your church's library may also include songs shared by other churches — those show **"Published by {church}"**.

(Adding and presenting songs is a leader feature — see [section 46](#46-managing-songs--the-presenter).)

## 22. Booking a room

Click **Room Booking** in the sidebar.

1. **Select a location** — pick the room (each shows its type, e.g. **Hall**, **Classroom**, and features like **Projector**, **AC**, **Wi-Fi**).
2. **Select a date** on the calendar. Existing bookings for that day are listed, so you can see what's free (*"No bookings for this day"* means it's all yours).
3. Click **New Booking** (the **+**) and fill in:
   - **Booking Title** (and optional **Title (Arabic)**)
   - **Date**, **From** and **To** times
   - **Notes** (optional) and **Show on dashboard** if it should appear on the church's daily list
4. Click **Book**. Success shows *"Booking created"*.
5. If someone beat you to it you'll see **"Time conflict"** — *"This time slot is already booked"* — pick a different time.

**Your bookings:** open **My Bookings** (the page tab) to see **Upcoming** and **Past** bookings. To cancel one, click **Cancel Booking** and confirm in the dialog (*"Are you sure you want to cancel this booking?"* → **Cancel Booking** / **Keep Booking**).

## 23. Church Needs — helping other churches

**Church Needs** (sidebar) is a marketplace where churches across Ekklesia help each other with practical needs — furniture, supplies, food, volunteers, and more.

**Browsing needs:**
1. The **All Needs** tab lists needs posted by other churches. Each card shows the need's title, category (e.g. **Furniture**, **Medical**, **Building Materials**), urgency (**Low** → **Critical**), status (**Open**, **In Progress**, **Fulfilled**, **Closed**), and which church posted it.
2. Filter by **Country**, **Category**, **Urgency**, **Status**, or search (*"Search needs..."*).

**Offering help:**
1. Open a need and click **Offer Help**.
2. Write **Your Message** (what you can provide) and click **Send Offer** — *"Your offer has been sent"*.
3. You can't respond to your own church's needs (*"You cannot respond to your own church's need"*), and each church can respond once (*"Your church has already responded"*).

**Posting a need (needs permission):**
1. Click **Post a Need**.
2. Fill in **Need Title** (+ Arabic), **Description**, **Category**, **Quantity**, **Urgency**, contact details (**Contact Name**, **Contact Phone**, **Contact Email**), optional **Expires At** and an image (**Upload Image**).
3. Submit — *"Need posted successfully"*.

**Managing your needs:** the **Your Needs** tab shows what your church posted, with a **Responses** count. Open one to **Accept** or **Decline** each offer. Accepting opens a **Messages** thread with the helping church — type in *"Type a message..."* and press **Send message** to coordinate. When the need is met, **Mark as Fulfilled**.

The **Messages** tab collects all your cross-church conversations in one place.

## 24. Groups — finding and joining one

Click **Groups** in the sidebar.

- **Your Groups** — groups you already belong to; click one to see its page.
- **Open Groups** — if you're not in a group: *"You're not part of a group yet. Here are groups open for new members:"* Each card shows the group type (**Small Group**, **Youth**, **Women**, **Men**, **Families**, **Prayer**), meeting day, and leader.
- Click **Request to Join** on a group you like. You'll see *"Join request sent!"* and the button becomes **Request Pending** until the leader approves you.
- No open groups? *"There are no groups open for new members right now. We'll keep you updated when one becomes available."*

## 25. Church invitations

If another church adds you as a member (say, a sister congregation), Ekklesia never moves you silently — you get an invitation card on your dashboard: **"Church invitations"** — *"{church} invited you to join"*.

- Click **Accept** to join that church (*"Invitation accepted"*) — it will then appear in your church switcher.
- Click **Decline** to say no thanks (*"Invitation declined"*).

## 26. My Group page

As a group leader, your **Groups** page leads to your group's home. Group leaders also get a richer dashboard with **Quick Actions** (**Start Gathering**, **View My Visitors**), an **At-Risk Members** card, and **Active Prayer Requests**.

Your group page shows:

- **Upcoming Gathering** — the next scheduled meeting, with **"Open to log attendance →"**.
- **At-Risk Members** — members who've been missing meetings, each with a **View Profile** link. Reach out to them!
- **Meeting History** — past gatherings and how many *attended*.

## 27. Scheduling a gathering

1. On your group page, click **+ New Gathering**.
2. On **New Gathering**, fill in:
   - **Date and Time** (required)
   - **Location** (e.g. "Church hall")
   - **Topic / Lesson** (e.g. "Meeting topic") — defaults to **"Group Meeting"** if left blank
   - **Notes** (optional)
3. Click **Create Gathering**. You'll see *"Gathering created"*.

The gathering now appears as **Scheduled** and shows up in your members' dashboards under "Upcoming This Week".

## 28. Taking attendance

Open the gathering (from **Upcoming Gathering** or **Meeting History**).

**What you'll see:** the **Attendance** section with your full roster and a live counter — **"{present} present out of {total}"**. There are two views, toggled at the top:

- **List** view — every member in a list. *"Click on a name to change status"* — each click cycles through the four statuses: **Present** → **Late** → **Excused** → **Absent**.
- **Cards** view — one big card per member, made for phones: **swipe right for Present, swipe left for Absent**. Made a mistake? Tap **Undo**.

**Saving:**
1. Click **Save Attendance** anytime to store what you've marked so far (*"Attendance saved"*). You can come back and continue.
2. When the meeting's over, click **Complete Gathering ✓**. This finalizes the record — you'll see *"Gathering completed and absences verified"* — and the gathering is marked **Completed**.

> ⚠️ **Complete the gathering when you're done.** Completing is what feeds attendance rates, at-risk detection, and each member's attendance history. A gathering left "Scheduled" doesn't count.

## 29. Group prayer requests & logging answers

Your group page has its own **Prayer Requests** section (separate from the church-wide prayer feed), showing *"{count} active"*.

**Adding a request:**
1. Click **Add Request**.
2. In **Add Prayer Request**, write the request (*"Write the prayer request here..."*).
3. Optionally tick **Private (Leader only)** for sensitive matters.
4. Click **Add** — *"Prayer request added"*.

**Logging an answered prayer:**
1. On a request card, click **Log Answer →**.
2. In **Log Prayer Answer**, optionally describe it — *"How did God answer? (optional)"*.
3. Click **Confirm Answer** — *"Answer logged"*. The request moves to the **Prayer Answers** section with an **Answered** badge. 🎉

## 30. Approving requests to join your group

When a member clicks "Request to Join" on your group, it appears under **Pending Join Requests** on your group page.

- Click **Approve** — they're added immediately (*"Member approved"*).
- Click **Reject** if the group isn't the right fit (*"Request rejected"*).

## 31. My assigned visitors

When church admins assign a newcomer to you for follow-up, they show up in **My Visitors** (sidebar).

**What you'll see:** **"My Assigned Visitors"** — *"Visitors assigned to you for follow-up"* — with each visitor's name, phone, and when they visited. (Empty? *"Visitors will appear here when assigned to you."*)

**Following up:**
1. Click **Call** to phone them straight from the app (mobile).
2. After you've talked, click **Log Contact**.
3. In **Log Contact with Visitor**, write what happened (*"Notes about the contact..."*) and click **Log Contact** — *"Contact logged"*.

Logging contact updates the visitor's status so the church office knows follow-up happened. You'll also get a notification ("Visitor Assigned") whenever a new visitor is assigned to you.

# Part V — Ministry Leader & Admin Guide

*Ministry leaders and super admins see the full admin sidebar. Everything below applies to both (except where noted).*

## 32. The admin dashboard

Your dashboard shows church-wide health at a glance:

- Four stat cards: **Active Members** (with growth: *"+{count} this month"*), **New Visitors** (with *"{count} need attention"* when follow-ups are due), **Attendance Rate**, and **Upcoming Events**.
- **Attendance Trend** — *"Weekly attendance rate — last 12 weeks"* as a line chart.
- **Visitor Pipeline** — a bar chart of visitors by stage: **New**, **Assigned**, **Contacted**, **Converted**.
- **Needs Attention** — *"Items requiring follow-up"*: overdue visitors, at-risk members. (All clear? *"Everything looks good!"*)
- **Upcoming This Week** — gatherings, events, and serving slots in the next 7 days.
- **Group Health** — a table of every active group: **Group**, **Leader**, **Members**, **Attendance**, **At Risk**, **Trend**.

## 33. The members directory

Click **Members** in the sidebar.

**What you'll see:** **"Members"** — *"{count} members in the church"* — with a search bar, filters, and the member table (on phones, a card list instead).

**Searching & filtering:**
1. Type in **"Search by name or email..."** — matching members appear instantly in a dropdown as you type; click one to open their profile.
2. Or use the **All Roles** / **All Statuses** dropdowns and the **Search** button to filter the table.
3. The table shows **Member**, **Role**, **Status**, **Phone**, **Join Date**, and a **View** link per row. Large churches paginate — **"Page {x} of {y}"** with **Previous** / **Next**.

**Status badges you'll see:** **Active**, **Inactive**, **Needs Follow-up** (at-risk), **Visitor** — plus **Pending claim** for members who were added by a leader but haven't signed in yet.

> 💡 Phone numbers may be hidden depending on your church's privacy setting ([section 53](#53-privacy--who-sees-phone-numbers)).

## 34. Adding a member

1. On **Members**, click **Add member** (top right).
2. A dialog opens: **"Add a member"** — *"Add someone by name. With a phone number they can sign in via WhatsApp and claim their account."* Fill in:
   - **First name** and **Last name** (required)
   - **First name (Arabic)** / **Last name (Arabic)** (optional)
   - **Phone (optional)** — international format (e.g. +201234567890). The hint: *"They'll use this to sign in with WhatsApp."*
3. Click **Add member**.

**What happens:**
- **With a phone** → *"Member added — They can now sign in with their phone via WhatsApp."* The person appears in your directory with a **Pending claim** badge. The moment they sign in with that phone number ([section 5](#5-signing-in)), the account becomes theirs — no approval queue needed.
- **Without a phone** → *"Added as a managed record (no phone)."* Perfect for children or elderly members who'll never log in; you keep their attendance and records anyway.
- **Phone already belongs to someone in another church** → *"Invitation sent — They already belong to another church; they'll join once they accept your invitation."* (Consent first, always — see [section 25](#25-church-invitations).)

> ⚠️ *"This person is already a member of your church"* means the phone number matches an existing member — search for them instead.

## 35. Managing one member's profile

Click **View** on any member (or pick them from search). The **Member Profile** page has six tabs:

- **Info** — their contact details and personal info. (*"Member has not completed their profile yet"* if they haven't onboarded.)
- **Spiritual Milestones** — click **Add Milestone** to record one: choose a **Type** (**Baptism**, **Salvation**, **Bible Reading Plan**, **Leadership Training**, **Marriage**, **Other**), a **Title**, optional **Date** and **Notes**, then **Save** — *"Spiritual milestone added"*.
- **Involvement** — their whole church life in one place, with sub-tabs: **Overview** (stat tiles like **Events Served**, **Serving Signups**, **Active Groups**, **Events Registered**), **Event Service**, **Serving**, **Groups & Ministries**, and **Registrations**. From here you can also directly **Add to Group** or **Add to Ministry**.
- **Outreach** — their home-visit history ([section 47](#47-outreach--home-visits--assignments)).
- **Connection Summary** — a quick relational overview.
- **Manage** — the admin tab:
  - **Change Role** — pick **Member**, **Group Leader**, **Ministry Leader**, or **Super Admin** and click **Save** — *"Role changed successfully"*.
  - **Permission Overrides** — per-person permission toggles ([section 52](#52-role-permissions--per-member-overrides)).

> ⚠️ **Changing roles changes access immediately.** Making someone a Ministry Leader gives them the entire admin sidebar. Making someone a Super Admin gives them everything, including Settings.

## 36. The visitors queue

Click **Visitors Queue** in the sidebar — this is your church's front door.

**What you'll see:** **"Visitors List"** — *"Manage and follow up with new visitors"*:
- The **Visitor Pipeline** bar — how many visitors are **New** → **Assigned** → **Contacted** → **Became a Member**.
- A red alert when follow-ups are late: **"{count} visitors overdue (SLA: 48h)"**.
- Stat cards: **Total**, **New**, **Assigned**, **Overdue**.
- Filter chips (**All** / **New** / **Assigned** / **Contacted**) and a search box (*"Search by visitor name or phone..."*).

**The visitor workflow:**
1. **Assign** — click **Assign** on a new visitor. In **"Assign Visitor to Leader"**, pick a leader from **"Choose a leader"** and confirm — *"Visitor assigned"*. The leader is notified and the visitor moves to **Assigned**.
2. **Log Contact** — after someone reaches out, click **Log Contact**, write your notes, confirm — *"Contact logged"*. Status becomes **Contacted**.
3. **Convert to Member** — when they're ready to join, click **Convert to Member**. The dialog warns: *"A new account will be created for this visitor. Are you sure?"* Click **Confirm** — *"Visitor converted to member"* — and they appear in your members directory.

> 💡 The **SLA** (from your church settings, default 48h) is a promise: every visitor contacted within that window. The **Overdue** card and red alert tell you when you're breaking it.

## 37. The visitor QR code & form settings

**The QR code** — Settings → **QR Code** (or **QR Code** in the sidebar):
- You'll see **"Visitor QR Code"** — *"Print this code and place it at the church entrance. Visitors can scan it to register their visit."*
- Buttons: **Copy Link** (the visit URL), **Download PNG** (the printable code — *"QR code downloaded"*).
- Print tip shown on the page: *"Print the code in A5 or A4 size and place it at the entrance for best results."*

**What visitors see when they scan:** a warm welcome page with your church's message, and a short form: **First Name**, **Last Name** (required), plus — depending on your form settings — **Phone Number**, **Email Address**, **Age Range**, **Occupation**, and **"How did you hear about us?"**. Submitting (**Register Visit**) lands them on **"Welcome to our family!"** — explaining that someone will contact them within 48 hours — and they instantly appear in your Visitors Queue as **New**.

**Customizing the form** — on the Visitors page, click **Preview Visitor Form**, or edit **Visitor Form Fields**: toggle each optional field on/off and mark fields as **Required**. First and last name are *"Always required"*. Click **Save Form Settings**.

## 38. Managing groups

Click **Groups** in the sidebar. As an admin you see **all** church groups — *"All church groups"* — searchable (*"Search by group name or leader..."*) and filterable by **All Types** / **All Ministries**.

**Creating a group:**
1. Click **New Group**.
2. On **"New Group"** fill in:
   - **Name (English)** (required) and **Name (Arabic)**
   - **Type** — Small Group, Youth, Women, Men, Families, Prayer, Other
   - **Ministry** — which ministry it belongs to
   - **Leader** — pick from your leaders (*"Choose a leader"*). Nobody suitable? Click **Register New Leader** — a dialog creates a leader account from just **Email**, **First Name**, **Last Name** (+ optional Arabic names & phone); they receive a password-reset email to set their own password, and existing members are simply promoted (*"Existing member promoted to group leader"*).
   - **Co-Leader** (optional)
   - **Meeting Day**, **Meeting Time**, **Meeting Location**, **Frequency** (Weekly / Every two weeks / Monthly / Irregular)
   - **Max Members** and **Admission Status** — toggle **Open for joining** so members can request to join
3. Click **Save Group** — *"Group created"*.

**The group detail page** shows the leader (with phone), meeting details, and the full roster. From **Members**, click **Add Member** to search and add anyone (*"Search for a member..."*), or **Remove** (with a confirmation: *"Are you sure you want to remove this member from the group?"*). Click **Edit** to change any group setting.

## 39. Managing ministries

Click **Ministries** in the sidebar — *"Manage church ministries and services"*.

**Creating one:** click **Add Ministry** and fill in **Name (English)**, **Name (Arabic)**, **Description**, and the **Responsible Leader**, then **Save** — *"Ministry created"*. You can also upload a **Ministry Photo** (max 5MB) when editing.

**A ministry's detail page** brings together:

- **Notify** — send a message to every ministry member at once (title + body, Arabic and English) — *"Notification sent to {count} members"*.

## 40. Creating and managing events

Click **Events** in the sidebar. Admins see **"Manage church events and activities"** with two buttons: **From Template** and **New Event**.

**Creating an event — the 7-step wizard:**
1. Click **New Event** → **"Create Event"**.
2. **Step 1 — Title & Type:** type the **Title** and pick an **Event Type** (**Service**, **Conference**, **Retreat**, **Workshop**, **Social**, **Outreach**, **Other**). Click **Next**.
3. **Step 2 — When?:** set **Start Date & Time** (required) and optionally **End Date & Time**. Click **Next**.
4. **Step 3 — Where?:** enter a **Location** and **Capacity** (*"Leave empty for unlimited"*). Click **Next**.
5. **Step 4 — Details:** write a **Description**, then two toggles: **Public event (visible to everyone)** and **Registration required** (turn this on if people must RSVP). Click **Next**.
6. **Step 5 — Audience:** **"Who can see this event?"** — **All Members** (*"Visible to everyone"*) or **Specific Groups** (*"Only selected groups/ministries"*). Choosing specific groups also offers **Hide from non-invited** to make it completely invisible to everyone else. Click **Next**.
7. **Step 6 — Service Needs:** optionally click **Add Service Need** to request volunteer teams — pick a **Ministry** or **Group**, set **Volunteers Needed**, and add **Notes** (e.g. "2 camera operators, 1 sound engineer"). Click **Next**.
8. **Step 7 — Review:** check the summary and click **Create Event** — *"Event created"*. You land on the event's admin page (new events start as **Draft**).

**The event admin page** (click any event) contains:
- **Edit** and a red **delete** button (confirmation: *"Are you sure you want to delete '{title}'? This action cannot be undone."*).
- **Run of Show** — the service program, segment by segment, with durations.
- **Staffing** — volunteer assignment per service need: each shows **"{assigned} of {needed} assigned"** and a status (**Fully Staffed** / **Partially Staffed** / **Needs Staffing**). Click **Manage Staffing** to open **Event Staffing** — search members, click **Assign Member**, set their **Role / Position** (e.g. "Camera Operator, Vocals, Sound"). Volunteers get notified and can **Confirm** or **Decline** from their own dashboards (**My Service Assignments**).
- **Service Requests** — delegate finding people: click **Request Service Lead** to ask a specific member to fill a role (e.g. "Sound Engineer"); they **Accept**, **Decline**, or **Assign Someone** else.
- **Registrations** — the RSVP list with **Total** / **Checked In** / **Pending** counters and a search box. Use it as your check-in desk on event day.

## 41. Event templates

For services you run every week, build them once as a template. Click **Templates** in the sidebar — *"Reusable blueprints for recurring events"*.

**Creating a template:** click **New Template** and set:
- **Template Name** (e.g. "Sunday Morning Service") and **Default Event Title**
- **Event Defaults** — type, location, times
- **Schedule** — a **Recurrence** (**None (one-time)**, **Weekly**, **Every two weeks**, **Monthly**) with a **Day of week** and **Default Start/End Time**; the card then shows *"Next: {date}"*
- **Run of Show** — click **Add Segment** for each part of the service (**Segment Title** e.g. "Worship, Sermon, Offering", **Duration (minutes)**, optional **Ministry** assignment and **Notes**). Reorder with **Move Up** / **Move Down**. The builder totals it up: **Total Duration**.
- **Role Presets** — standard volunteer roles (**Role Name** + **Count**)
- **Custom Fields** — extra per-event fields (**Text**, **Number**, **Dropdown**, **Yes/No**)
Click **Create Template**.

**Using it:** on the Events page click **From Template** → pick the template (**Use**) → the wizard opens pre-filled with everything ("will be copied") → set the date → **Create Event**. A full Sunday service in under a minute.

## 49. Sending notifications to your church

Click **Send Notifications** in the sidebar — *"Compose and send targeted notifications to church members"*.

1. **Select Audience** — choose who receives it:
   - **Entire Church**
   - **By Role** (Members / Group Leaders / Ministry Leaders / Admins)
   - **By Group** or **By Ministry** (pick specific ones)
   - **By Status** (Active / At-Risk / Inactive)
   - **By Gender**
   - **Visitors (WhatsApp only)** — reach visitors who left a phone number

4. A confirmation dialog stops you from accidents: **"Confirm Send"** — *"This notification will be sent to {total} people. This action cannot be undone."* Click **Send**.
5. Success: *"Sent to {count} recipients"*.

> 💡 Group leaders and ministry leaders can send too, but only to their own people — the page tells them: *"You can send to members of your groups"* / *"...of your ministries and their groups"*.

## 50. Approving church join requests

## 52. Role permissions & per-member overrides

**Church-wide role defaults** — Settings → **Role Permissions**:
- You'll see **"Role Permission Defaults"** — *"Configure the default permissions for each role. Changes apply to all users with that role (unless they have individual overrides)."*
- Pick a role tab, toggle its permissions (view members, manage events, view visitors, and so on), then **Save Permissions** — *"Permissions saved"*.
- Note at the bottom: *"Super admins always have full permissions."* You can't lock yourself out.

**Per-member overrides** — two ways in:
1. **Members → (member) → Manage tab → Permission Overrides**, or the **Permissions** page (search members).
2. Toggle individual permissions on or off for just that person — *"Highlighted toggles differ from the role default."* This is how you give one trusted member extra access (say, viewing visitors) without promoting their role.

## 53. Privacy — who sees phone numbers

Settings → **Privacy** → **"Member contact visibility"** — *"Choose who can see member phone numbers in the member directory."* Three options:

- **Everyone with directory access** — anyone who can open the directory sees phones.
- **Leaders only** — *"Only ministry leaders and administrators can see phone numbers."*
- **Administrators only** — *"Only super administrators can see phone numbers."* ← the default; the most private.

Click your choice — *"Privacy settings saved"*. This applies everywhere member phones appear (directory, group pages, ministry pages), not just the directory. People always see their own number.

## 54. Notification channels (WhatsApp)

Settings → **Notifications** → **"Notification Channels"**:

- The page notes: *"In-app push notifications are free and always on for every member."*
- The toggle **"Send notifications over WhatsApp"** controls the paid channel: *"WhatsApp delivery incurs a per-message cost. When off, members still receive free in-app push notifications."*
- Flip it and you'll see *"Notification settings saved"*.

> 💡 Leave WhatsApp off until your church decides it's worth the per-message cost. Nobody misses anything — they still get every notification in-app.

## 58. Limits, defaults & good-to-know details

- **Lists show 25 items per page** (members, notifications, and so on) — use the pagination arrows.
- **Images max 5MB** — profile photos, ministry photos, song backgrounds, notification images. Larger files are rejected with a clear message.
- **Passwords: at least 6 characters.**
- **Phone numbers must include the country code** (e.g. +20…, +961…, +964…) — that's how WhatsApp sign-in and notifications find you.
- **New events start as Draft** — they're only visible to members once published.
- **Visitor SLA defaults to 48 hours** — the deadline for contacting each new visitor before they're flagged overdue.
- **Member phone privacy defaults to "Administrators only"** — the most private option.
- **WhatsApp notifications default to OFF** (in-app push is always on and free).
- **One phone number = one person** across all of Ekklesia — it's how accounts are claimed and deduplicated.
- **Song slides split on blank lines** — press Enter twice between verses when adding lyrics.
- **The "Giving / Finance" module is not yet available** — it's under development and hidden from the app.
- **Numbers and dates localize** — in Arabic mode you'll see Arabic-Indic numerals (١٢٣) and Arabic dates; phone numbers always stay left-to-right for readability.

## 59. Troubleshooting

**"I can't sign in with my email."** Double-check for typos, then use **Forgot password?** ([section 6](#6-forgot-your-password)). Remember the reset message shows either way — check the spam folder too.

**"The WhatsApp code never arrives."** Confirm the number includes your country code and has WhatsApp. Then tap **Change number** and resend. Codes expire — always use the newest one.

**"I registered my church but can't get in."** You're seeing *"Your church is under review"* — that's the approval step ([section 3](#3-registering-your-church-for-pastors)). You'll have access as soon as an operator approves it.

**"I joined a church but I'm not in it yet."** Joining a *second* church needs leader approval — your request is sitting in their **Join Requests** page. Ask a church leader to approve it.

**"I can't see the admin pages my pastor sees."** Menus follow your role ([section 2](#2-the-four-roles--and-what-each-one-can-do)). If you should have access, ask a super admin to change your role (**Members → your profile → Manage → Change Role**).

**"A member's phone number isn't showing."** That's the privacy setting doing its job ([section 53](#53-privacy--who-sees-phone-numbers)). Ask your super admin if leaders should see phones.

**"My group's attendance rate looks wrong."** Make sure leaders press **Complete Gathering ✓** after each meeting — only completed gatherings count ([section 28](#28-taking-attendance)).

**"An event isn't visible to members."** Check three things: it's **Published** (not Draft), it's in the future, and its **Audience** isn't limited to specific groups ([section 40](#40-creating-and-managing-events)).

**"The page looks stuck or odd."** Refresh the browser. Ekklesia is a web app — a refresh gets you the latest version. If you're offline you'll see the offline page; reconnect and try again.
